Humidity

Relative humidity (RH), referred to as "humidity" here, is the amount of moisture in the air divided by how much moisture the air can hold. Back to the Latitude vs. Humidity plot, polynomial regression ( / ) with relatively low (deg=5, red dashed line) and high degrees (deg=25, magenta solid line) both suggest there be RH peaks around 10oS and 60oN and grooves around 40oS and 35oN.

Many factors are able to affect RH, with temperature sitting at the top hierarchy. Inspired by the Latitude vs. Temperature plot, it is easy to conclude the 10oS peak to be most likely high-temperature driven. Global atmospheric circulation is another factor that counts in this Latitude vs. Humidity plot as relatively low atmospheric pressure enables it to hold more moisture in the air. Coincidentally, both 10oS (Equatorial LPZ) and 60oN (Subpolar LPZ) locate in low pressure zones (LPZ) whereas 40oS and 35oN are in Subtropical high pressure zone (HPZ). Moreover, the cold, dense Arctic polar water descending along the ocean bottom towards the equator might be an individual contributor to the high humidity around 60oN by stirring the surface water to vaporize to the air.
